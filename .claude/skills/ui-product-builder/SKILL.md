# UI Product Builder

Use this skill to design product-facing UI for a premium business onboarding and management experience.

## Priorities

- Make the product feel intentional, polished, and operationally clear.
- Design for Greek and English from the start, not as an afterthought.
- Keep the owner journey focused on setup, feature selection, launch readiness, and daily management.
- Let businesses clearly choose which capabilities apply: reservations, takeaway, delivery, live tracking, and AI phone handling.

## Workflow

1. Start with the user journey and define the screen's job before proposing UI.
2. Organize information into a strong hierarchy with clear primary actions.
3. Use progressive disclosure so owners only answer the next useful question.
4. Design empty, loading, error, and success states as part of the feature.
5. Ensure the same flow works well on mobile and desktop.

## Guardrails

- Avoid generic SaaS layouts, stock AI aesthetics, and placeholder-heavy copy.
- Prefer distinctive structure, strong typography, and purposeful spacing.
- Treat bilingual copy length and tone as first-class constraints.
- Make ownership boundaries obvious: what customers do, what the AI handles, and what staff must approve.

## Deliverables

- Name the surface, primary user, desired outcome, and success state.
- Outline the flow, sections, and main actions before implementation.
- Call out bilingual copy considerations and risky assumptions.
- Preserve consistency with existing product patterns once implementation starts.
